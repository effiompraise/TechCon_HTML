About TechCon 2024

History:

TechCon, a premier tech conference, was founded in 2010 by a group of tech enthusiasts as a small meetup in San Francisco, focusing on emerging trends in technology. Over the years, it expanded to a full-day conference, introducing keynote speakers, panel discussions, and hackathons, covering topics like mobile development, cloud computing, AI, cybersecurity, and data science. By 2020, TechCon had grown to a global audience of over 5,000 attendees, despite going virtual due to the pandemic, and by 2024, it had become a sold-out event with 2,500 attendees, featuring renowned speakers, interactive workshops, and a showcase of cutting-edge technologies, cementing its position as a leading platform for tech innovation and collaboration.

Our Mission:

The mission of TechCon is to "Empower Innovation, Connect the Future" by providing a platform for technologists, innovators, and industry leaders to share knowledge, showcase cutting-edge technologies, and collaborate on shaping the future of technology.

Past Speakers:

We've had the privilege of hosting some of the most influential minds in the tech industry at TechCon. Here are a few notable speakers from previous years:

  • Dr. Fei-Fei Li

    Dr. Fei-Fei Li is a renowned computer scientist and director of the Stanford Artificial Intelligence Lab (SAIL). Her work has revolutionized the field of artificial intelligence, and she has made significant contributions to the development of AI for social good.

  • Andrew Ng

    Andrew Ng is a pioneer in the field of artificial intelligence and the founder of Coursera. He has made significant contributions to the development of deep learning and has been a driving force behind the adoption of AI in various industries.

  • Reshma Saujani

    Reshma Saujani is the founder of Girls Who Code, a non-profit organization dedicated to closing the gender gap in technology. Her work has inspired thousands of young women to pursue careers in tech, and she has been a vocal advocate for diversity and inclusion in the industry.
